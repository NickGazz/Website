Improving Children's Literacy

Early childhood education is very important and starts before children even start school at the age of 4 or 5, yet many students are not starting off with as strong of a base as they should. For that reason my senior thesis is looking at why that is and how product design can play a role in improving children's literacy.

By age 3 children from professional class families know about 11,000 words, which is more than twice as many as their less privileged peers. By the age of 4 children from professional class families hear 3 times as may words, which is about 30 million more words, than children from welfare families.
This little early literacy experience has been proven to lead to poor early phonological awareness, slow vocabulary development, and eventually academic failure, which perpetuates the cycle.

Early ideation allowed me explore different methods of exposing children to a larger vocabulary in both quality and quantity. Some early thoughts included an e-reader, speaker, or wearable, before settling on a companion style device that can tell stories and have conversations with the child.

Moving forward I intend to settle on a final form that has a screen to promote visual interactions that helps with learning language through associating what is heard with what is visible, and the object will need to have a speaker for the auditory interaction. Additionally the final device will need to have a playful feeling to it so that the child using it finds it enjoyable to use and wants to engage with it.
