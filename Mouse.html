Sustainable Computer Mouse

The computer mouse is a product that is very familar and extremly prevelant in society. Most mice are ephemeral and easily disposed of. For that reason, it is an excellent example of a computer peripheral that can be redesigned and improved in order to promote a more sustainable approach to electronics.

Electronics in landfills leach toxins like lead, mercury, and cadmium into the soil and water

Only 7.8 out of the 82.2 Million mice and keyboards disposed in 2010 were recycled

The picture on the left shows what a current circuit in a computer mouse looks like. It contains many components and hazardous chemicals, and cannot be easily recycled. The photo on the right shows a circuit board being dissolved in water. This is possible because of a growing new technology known as transient electronics. These are circuits that are biodegradable, and produce a harmless liquid when dissolved at the end of their useful life. This technology is a key component in the design of my sustainable mouse.
